## Grammar Fundamentals

### Parts of Speech

| Part of Speech | Function | Examples |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| Noun | names a person, place, thing, idea | dog, city, happiness |
| Pronoun | replaces a noun | I, you, he, she, it, they |
| Verb | expresses action or state | run, is, become |
| Adjective | describes a noun | big, beautiful, smart |
| Adverb | modifies verb, adjective, or adverb | quickly, very, well |
| Preposition | shows relationship | in, on, at, by, with |
| Conjunction | connects words/clauses | and, but, or, because |
| Interjection | expresses emotion | oh, wow, ouch |
| Article | specifies noun | a, an, the |

### Sentence Structure

**Basic patterns:**

- S + V: She sleeps.
- S + V + O: She reads books.
- S + V + C: She is happy.
- S + V + IO + DO: She gave him a book.
- S + V + O + C: She made him happy.

### Word Order

**Standard order:** Subject + Verb + Object + Manner + Place + Time

Example: She studied English hard at school yesterday.

**Adverb placement:**
- Frequency adverbs before main verb: I always eat breakfast.
- After "be": She is always late.
- At beginning/end for emphasis: Sometimes I work late.

## Tenses

### Present Simple

**Form:** base verb (+ s/es for 3rd person singular)

**Usage:**
- Facts and general truths: Water boils at 100°C.
- Habits and routines: I wake up at 7 AM.
- Schedules: The train leaves at 6 PM.
- Permanent situations: She lives in New York.

**Markers:** always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/year

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I work | I do not (don't) work | Do I work? |
| He works | He does not (doesn't) work | Does he work? |

### Present Continuous (Progressive)

**Form:** am/is/are + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Actions happening now: I am reading a book.
- Temporary situations: She is staying with friends.
- Future arrangements: We are meeting tomorrow.
- Annoying habits (with always): He is always complaining.

**Markers:** now, right now, at the moment, currently, today

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I am working | I am not working | Am I working? |
| He is working | He is not working | Is he working? |

**Non-action (stative) verbs** - usually not used in continuous:
- Mental states: know, believe, understand, remember, forget, think (opinion), suppose
- Emotions: love, hate, like, prefer, want, need, wish
- Senses: see, hear, smell, taste, feel
- Possession: have, own, belong, possess
- Others: be, seem, appear, cost, weigh, contain

### Present Perfect

**Form:** have/has + past participle (V3)

**Usage:**
- Past actions with present relevance: I have lost my keys.
- Life experiences: She has visited Paris twice.
- Recent actions: He has just arrived.
- Unfinished actions: I have lived here for 5 years.

**Markers:** just, already, yet, ever, never, recently, lately, so far, up to now, since, for

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I have worked | I have not (haven't) worked | Have I worked? |
| He has worked | He has not (hasn't) worked | Has he worked? |

**Since vs For:**
- Since + point in time: since Monday, since 2020
- For + period of time: for two days, for years

### Present Perfect Continuous

**Form:** have/has + been + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Actions started in past continuing to now: I have been waiting for an hour.
- Recent continuous actions with visible results: She has been crying.
- Emphasis on duration: They have been working all day.

**Markers:** for, since, all day/morning, how long

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I have been working | I haven't been working | Have I been working? |
| He has been working | He hasn't been working | Has he been working? |

### Past Simple

**Form:** verb + ed (regular) or V2 (irregular)

**Usage:**
- Completed actions in past: I visited Rome last year.
- Series of past actions: She woke up, had breakfast, and left.
- Past habits: When I was young, I played soccer.
- Past states: He lived in London for 10 years.

**Markers:** yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2010, when

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I worked | I did not (didn't) work | Did I work? |
| He worked | He did not (didn't) work | Did he work? |

### Past Continuous

**Form:** was/were + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Actions in progress at past time: At 8 PM, I was watching TV.
- Background actions: While she was cooking, he arrived.
- Parallel past actions: While I was reading, she was sleeping.
- Atmosphere/setting: The sun was shining, birds were singing.

**Markers:** while, when, as, at that time, all day yesterday

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I was working | I was not (wasn't) working | Was I working? |
| They were working | They were not (weren't) working | Were they working? |

### Past Perfect

**Form:** had + past participle (V3)

**Usage:**
- Action before another past action: When I arrived, she had left.
- Experience before past time: I had never seen snow before that trip.
- Reported speech: She said she had finished.

**Markers:** by the time, before, after, already, just, never, when

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I had worked | I had not (hadn't) worked | Had I worked? |
| He had worked | He had not (hadn't) worked | Had he worked? |

### Past Perfect Continuous

**Form:** had + been + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Duration of action before past event: I had been waiting for 2 hours when he called.
- Cause of past situation: She was tired because she had been working.

**Markers:** for, since, all day, how long, before

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I had been working | I hadn't been working | Had I been working? |

### Future Simple (Will)

**Form:** will + base verb

**Usage:**
- Predictions: It will rain tomorrow.
- Spontaneous decisions: I'll help you.
- Promises: I will call you.
- Facts about future: She will be 30 next year.

**Markers:** tomorrow, next week, soon, in the future

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I will work | I will not (won't) work | Will I work? |

### Future with "Going to"

**Form:** am/is/are + going to + base verb

**Usage:**
- Planned intentions: I am going to study medicine.
- Predictions based on evidence: Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I am going to work | I am not going to work | Am I going to work? |

### Future Continuous

**Form:** will + be + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Actions in progress at future time: At 8 PM, I will be watching TV.
- Polite questions: Will you be using the car tonight?

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I will be working | I won't be working | Will I be working? |

### Future Perfect

**Form:** will + have + past participle (V3)

**Usage:**
- Actions completed before future time: By 2025, I will have graduated.

**Markers:** by, by the time, before

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I will have worked | I won't have worked | Will I have worked? |

### Future Perfect Continuous

**Form:** will + have + been + verb-ing

**Usage:**
- Duration of action up to future point: By June, I will have been working here for 5 years.

| Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| I will have been working | I won't have been working | Will I have been working? |

## Sequence of Tenses

When the main clause is in past tense, the subordinate clause shifts back:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect |
| Will | Would |
| Can | Could |
| May | Might |

**Examples:**
- "I work here." → He said he worked there.
- "I am working." → He said he was working.
- "I have finished." → He said he had finished.
- "I will come." → He said he would come.

**Time/place changes:**
| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| now | then |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before |
| tomorrow | the next day |
| here | there |
| this | that |
| these | those |

## Modal Verbs

### Can / Could

**Can:**
- Ability: I can swim.
- Permission: Can I leave?
- Possibility: It can be dangerous.

**Could:**
- Past ability: I could swim when I was 5.
- Polite requests: Could you help me?
- Possibility: It could rain.

### May / Might

**May:**
- Permission (formal): May I come in?
- Possibility: She may be late.

**Might:**
- Possibility (less certain): He might come.
- Past permission: She asked if she might leave.

### Must / Have to

**Must:**
- Obligation (speaker's opinion): You must study.
- Strong probability: He must be tired.

**Have to:**
- External obligation: I have to wear a uniform.
- Necessity: You have to have a passport.

**Mustn't vs Don't have to:**
- Mustn't = prohibition: You mustn't smoke here.
- Don't have to = no necessity: You don't have to come.

### Should / Ought to

- Advice: You should see a doctor.
- Expectation: She should arrive soon.
- Criticism of past: You should have called.

### Would

- Past habit: He would always help.
- Polite requests: Would you open the window?
- Conditionals: If I were rich, I would travel.

### Shall

- Offers/suggestions: Shall I help you?
- Future (formal British): I shall return.

## Passive Voice

**Form:** be + past participle (V3)

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | writes | is written |
| Present Continuous | is writing | is being written |
| Present Perfect | has written | has been written |
| Past Simple | wrote | was written |
| Past Continuous | was writing | was being written |
| Past Perfect | had written | had been written |
| Future Simple | will write | will be written |
| Modal | can write | can be written |

**Usage:**
- Unknown agent: My car was stolen.
- Unimportant agent: The building was constructed in 1990.
- Formal/scientific writing: The experiment was conducted.
- Focus on action/result: The report has been completed.

## Conditionals

### Zero Conditional

**Form:** If + Present Simple, Present Simple

**Usage:** General truths, facts

Example: If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

### First Conditional

**Form:** If + Present Simple, will + base verb

**Usage:** Real future possibility

Example: If it rains, I will stay home.

### Second Conditional

**Form:** If + Past Simple, would + base verb

**Usage:** Unreal present/future, hypothetical

Example: If I were rich, I would buy a yacht.

Note: "were" used for all persons (formal)

### Third Conditional

**Form:** If + Past Perfect, would + have + V3

**Usage:** Unreal past, regrets

Example: If I had studied, I would have passed.

### Mixed Conditionals

**Past condition, present result:**
If I had taken that job, I would be rich now.

**Present condition, past result:**
If I were braver, I would have asked her out.

## Reported Speech

### Statements

Direct: "I am tired."
Reported: She said (that) she was tired.

### Questions

**Yes/No questions:** Use if/whether
Direct: "Are you coming?"
Reported: He asked if I was coming.

**Wh-questions:** Keep question word, use statement order
Direct: "Where do you live?"
Reported: She asked where I lived.

### Commands/Requests

Use infinitive:
Direct: "Close the door."
Reported: He told me to close the door.

Direct: "Don't be late."
Reported: She told me not to be late.

## Articles

### Indefinite Articles: A / An

**Use "a" before consonant sounds:** a book, a university, a European
**Use "an" before vowel sounds:** an apple, an hour, an honest man

**Usage:**
- First mention: I saw a dog.
- One of many: She is a teacher.
- Per/each: twice a week, $10 an hour
- Exclamations: What a beautiful day!

### Definite Article: The

**Usage:**
- Previously mentioned: I saw a dog. The dog was big.
- Unique things: the sun, the moon, the Internet
- Superlatives: the best, the tallest
- Ordinals: the first, the second
- With "of": the center of town
- Instruments (playing): play the piano
- Nationalities (plural): the French, the Japanese
- Geographic features: the Pacific Ocean, the Alps, the Sahara
- Countries with "Republic/Kingdom/States": the USA, the UK

### No Article (Zero Article)

**Usage:**
- Plural/uncountable (general): Dogs are loyal. Water is essential.
- Meals (general): have breakfast/lunch/dinner
- Languages: speak English
- Sports/games: play soccer, play chess
- Academic subjects: study mathematics
- Most countries/cities: France, Paris
- Streets/parks: Fifth Avenue, Central Park
- Mountains (single): Mount Everest

## Prepositions

### Time Prepositions

| Preposition | Usage | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| at | specific time, holidays | at 5 PM, at noon, at Christmas |
| on | days, dates | on Monday, on July 4th |
| in | months, years, seasons, parts of day | in May, in 2020, in winter, in the morning |
| for | duration | for two hours, for years |
| since | starting point | since Monday, since 2010 |
| during | within a period | during the summer |
| by | no later than | by Friday |
| until/till | up to a point | until 5 PM |

### Place Prepositions

| Preposition | Usage | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| at | specific point/place | at the door, at school |
| in | inside enclosed space | in the room, in New York |
| on | surface | on the table, on the wall |
| above/over | higher than | above the clouds |
| below/under | lower than | under the table |
| between | two things | between us |
| among | more than two | among friends |
| beside/next to | at the side of | next to me |
| in front of | facing | in front of the house |
| behind | at the back of | behind the car |

### Movement Prepositions

| Preposition | Usage |
|-------------|-------|
| to | direction toward | go to school |
| into | entering | walk into the room |
| out of | leaving | get out of the car |
| through | from one side to another | walk through the park |
| across | from one side to another (surface) | swim across the river |
| along | following a line | walk along the street |
| toward(s) | in the direction of | drive toward the city |

### Common Preposition Combinations

**Adjective + Preposition:**
- afraid of, angry at/with, bad at, different from
- excited about, famous for, fond of, good at
- interested in, jealous of, kind to, married to
- proud of, responsible for, similar to, sorry for/about
- tired of, worried about

**Verb + Preposition:**
- agree with, apologize for, apply for, arrive at/in
- believe in, belong to, care about, consist of
- depend on, dream about/of, happen to, laugh at
- listen to, look at/for/after, pay for, succeed in
- talk about/to, think about/of, wait for, worry about

## Inversion

Inversion is when we reverse the normal subject-verb order for emphasis, formality, or in specific grammatical structures.

### Inversion after Negative/Restrictive Adverbials

When negative or restrictive expressions begin a sentence, subject and auxiliary verb are inverted:

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| Never | Never have I seen such beauty. |
| Rarely | Rarely does he complain. |
| Seldom | Seldom do we eat out. |
| Hardly...when | Hardly had I arrived when it started raining. |
| Scarcely...when | Scarcely had she finished when the phone rang. |
| No sooner...than | No sooner had I left than I realized my mistake. |
| Not only...but also | Not only did he apologize, but he also offered to pay. |
| Not until | Not until I got home did I notice the problem. |
| Only after | Only after the meeting did I understand. |
| Only when | Only when you try will you succeed. |
| Only by | Only by working hard can you achieve your goals. |
| Only if | Only if you study will you pass. |
| Under no circumstances | Under no circumstances should you open that door. |
| On no account | On no account must you tell anyone. |
| In no way | In no way was this acceptable. |
| At no time | At no time did I feel unsafe. |
| Nowhere | Nowhere will you find a better deal. |
| Little | Little did he know what awaited him. |
| So + adjective | So beautiful was the view that I took a photo. |
| Such | Such was the damage that repairs took months. |

### Inversion in Conditionals (Formal)

| Standard | Inverted (Formal) |
|----------|-------------------|
| If I had known... | Had I known... |
| If he were here... | Were he here... |
| If it should happen... | Should it happen... |

**Examples:**
- Had I realized the consequences, I would have acted differently.
- Were she to accept, we would be delighted.
- Should you need assistance, please call.

### Inversion after "So/Neither/Nor"

Agreement with previous statements:
- "I love coffee." → "So do I." (= I love coffee too)
- "I don't like spiders." → "Neither/Nor do I."
- "She has finished." → "So has he."

### Inversion with "Here/There"

With verbs of movement/position (not with pronouns):
- Here comes the bus. (NOT: Here the bus comes.)
- There goes my last chance.
- BUT: Here it comes. (pronoun = no inversion)

## Cleft Sentences

Cleft sentences split information to emphasize a particular element.

### It-Clefts

**Structure:** It + be + focused element + that/who clause

**Used to emphasize:**
- Subject: It was John who broke the window. (not someone else)
- Object: It was the window that John broke. (not something else)
- Time: It was yesterday that I saw her. (not another day)
- Place: It was in Paris that they met. (not elsewhere)
- Reason: It was because of the rain that we stayed home.

### What-Clefts (Pseudo-clefts)

**Structure:** What + clause + be + focused element

**Examples:**
- What I need is a vacation.
- What happened was (that) everyone left early.
- What she did was (to) call the police.
- What I don't understand is why he lied.

### All-Clefts

**Structure:** All + clause + be + focused element

**Examples:**
- All I want is some peace and quiet.
- All you need to do is sign here.
- All that matters is your happiness.

### The thing/person/place/reason-Clefts

- The thing I hate most is dishonesty.
- The person who helped me was a stranger.
- The place where I feel happiest is home.
- The reason why I left was the noise.

### Reversed What-Clefts

Put the focus first for dramatic effect:
- A complete disaster is what it was.
- Sleep is what I need.

## Participle Clauses

Participle clauses use present participle (-ing), past participle (-ed/V3), or perfect participle (having + V3) to combine information.

### Present Participle Clauses

**Active meaning, simultaneous or sequential actions:**

- Walking home, I saw an accident. (= While I was walking)
- The man standing by the door is my uncle. (= who is standing)
- Being tired, she went to bed early. (= Because she was tired)
- Knowing the answer, he raised his hand. (= Because he knew)

### Past Participle Clauses

**Passive meaning:**

- Written in 1605, the play is still popular. (= which was written)
- Shocked by the news, she couldn't speak. (= Because she was shocked)
- Built of stone, the house has survived centuries.
- Seen from above, the city looks tiny.

### Perfect Participle Clauses

**Action completed before the main clause:**

- Having finished dinner, we went for a walk. (= After we had finished)
- Having been warned, he was careful. (= Because he had been warned)
- Not having received a reply, I called again.

### Participle Clauses with Conjunctions

You can keep the conjunction for clarity:
- While waiting for the bus, I read a book.
- When asked about it, she denied everything.
- Although knowing the risks, he proceeded.
- If heated, water evaporates.

### Common Errors to Avoid

**Dangling participles** (the subject must be the same):
- WRONG: Walking down the street, the trees were beautiful.
- RIGHT: Walking down the street, I noticed the beautiful trees.

## Advanced Relative Clauses

### Reduced Relative Clauses

Omit the relative pronoun + be:
- The man (who is) standing there is my boss.
- The book (which was) written by Orwell is famous.
- The issues (that are) being discussed are complex.

### Prepositions in Relative Clauses

**Formal:** preposition before relative pronoun
**Informal:** preposition at the end

| Formal | Informal |
|--------|----------|
| The person to whom I spoke... | The person who I spoke to... |
| The house in which I grew up... | The house that I grew up in... |
| The reason for which he left... | The reason why he left... |

### Relative Clauses with Quantifiers

- Several of whom, many of which, none of whom, all of which, some of whom

**Examples:**
- I have three brothers, all of whom are married.
- She made several suggestions, none of which were practical.
- The students, most of whom passed, celebrated.

### Nominal Relative Clauses

What/whoever/whatever/whichever function as noun phrases:
- What you said upset me. (= The thing that...)
- Whoever arrives first can choose. (= The person who...)
- Whatever happens, I'll support you. (= No matter what...)
- Take whichever you prefer. (= any one that...)

### Sentential Relative Clauses

"Which" refers to an entire clause:
- He passed the exam, which surprised everyone.
- She arrived late, which annoyed the host.
- They won the match, which was unexpected.

## Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive expresses wishes, demands, suggestions, or hypothetical situations.

### Mandative Subjunctive (Base Form)

After verbs/adjectives of demanding, suggesting, recommending:
- I suggest (that) he **be** there on time.
- She insisted (that) the report **be** finished today.
- It is essential (that) everyone **attend**.
- They demanded (that) she **resign**.

**Common triggers:**
- Verbs: suggest, recommend, insist, demand, request, propose, require, advise, ask
- Adjectives: essential, important, vital, necessary, crucial, imperative, desirable

### Formulaic Subjunctive (Set Phrases)

- God bless you.
- Long live the King!
- Heaven forbid!
- Be that as it may...
- Suffice it to say...
- Come what may...
- If need be...
- As it were...

### Were-Subjunctive

Used in hypotheticals and conditionals:
- If I **were** you, I would accept. (not "was" in formal English)
- I wish I **were** taller.
- If she **were** here, she would know.
- He acts as if he **were** the boss.

## Causative Structures

Express having someone else do something for you.

### Have + Object + Past Participle

Something is done for you (by someone else):
- I **had my car repaired**. (Someone repaired it for me)
- She **had her hair cut** yesterday.
- We need to **have the house painted**.
- Have you **had your eyes tested** recently?

### Get + Object + Past Participle

More informal, often implies effort or difficulty:
- I finally **got my computer fixed**.
- Can you **get this translated** by tomorrow?
- She **got her application approved**.

### Have/Get + Object + Infinitive (without "to" for have / with "to" for get)

Cause someone to do something:
- I **had him check** the figures. (= I made him/asked him to)
- She **got him to help** with the project. (= She persuaded him to)

### Make + Object + Bare Infinitive

Force or cause:
- The teacher **made us rewrite** the essay.
- The movie **made me cry**.

### Let + Object + Bare Infinitive

Allow:
- She **let me use** her car.
- Don't **let him get** away.

### Experience (Negative Causative)

Have/get something happen (often negative):
- I **had my wallet stolen**. (It was stolen from me)
- She **got her heart broken**.

## Wish and If Only

Express regrets, desires, and hypothetical situations.

### Wish/If Only + Past Simple (Present Unreal)

Wish something were different NOW:
- I wish I **had** more time. (But I don't)
- If only she **were** here. (But she isn't)
- I wish I **knew** the answer. (But I don't know)
- If only I **didn't have** to work today.

### Wish/If Only + Past Perfect (Past Regret)

Wish something had been different in the PAST:
- I wish I **had studied** harder. (But I didn't)
- If only we **had left** earlier. (But we didn't)
- She wishes she **hadn't said** that. (But she did)

### Wish/If Only + Would (Future/Complaints)

Wish someone would change behavior or something would happen:
- I wish you **would stop** making noise.
- If only it **would stop** raining.
- I wish he **would listen** to me.

Note: Don't use "would" with the subject "I" (use "could"):
- I wish I **could** go. (NOT: I wish I would go)

### Wish + Could

Ability that doesn't exist:
- I wish I **could** play the piano.
- If only I **could** be there.

### It's (high/about) Time

- It's time we **left**. (= We should leave now)
- It's high time you **got** a job.
- It's about time she **learned** to drive.

### Would Rather/Sooner + Past

Preference about someone else's actions:
- I'd rather you **didn't mention** this to anyone.
- She'd sooner he **stayed** home.
- Would you rather I **came** back later?

## Ellipsis and Substitution

Avoiding repetition by omitting or replacing words.

### Ellipsis (Omission)

**After auxiliaries:**
- "Can you swim?" "Yes, I can (swim)."
- She said she would help, and she did (help).
- I haven't finished, but I will (finish) soon.

**After "to" (infinitive marker):**
- "Would you like to come?" "I'd love to (come)."
- I'll call if I need to (call).
- You don't have to apologize if you don't want to (apologize).

**In comparative structures:**
- She works harder than he does (work).
- I earn more than she does (earn).

### Substitution with "So" and "Not"

**With think, believe, hope, expect, suppose, be afraid, guess:**
- "Is she coming?" "I think so." / "I don't think so." / "I hope not."
- "Will it rain?" "I'm afraid so." / "I hope not."

**With say and tell:**
- "Is the meeting cancelled?" "They say so."

### Substitution with "Do So"

Replace a verb phrase:
- She asked me to call, and I did so immediately.
- If you wish to apply, please do so before Friday.

### Substitution with "One/Ones"

Replace countable nouns:
- "Which car?" "The red one."
- These shoes are nicer than those ones.
- I need a pen. Do you have one?

## Fronting and Emphasis

Moving elements to the front of a sentence for emphasis.

### Object Fronting

Move the object before the subject:
- **This book** I really enjoyed. (normal: I really enjoyed this book.)
- **That man** I cannot stand.
- **Such behavior** we will not tolerate.

### Complement Fronting

Move adjective/noun complements:
- **Lucky** he is to have such friends.
- **Strange** it may seem, but it's true.
- **Foolish** though it sounds, I believed him.

### Adverbial Fronting

For narrative effect or emphasis:
- **Down the street** came the parade.
- **Into the room** walked a stranger.
- **Away** flew the birds.

### Negative Fronting (with inversion)

See [[#Inversion]] section for details.

### "It is/was...that" for Emphasis

## Nominalization

Converting verbs/adjectives into nouns for formal or academic writing.

### Common Patterns

| Verb/Adjective | Nominalization |
|----------------|----------------|
| develop | development |
| analyze | analysis |
| discover | discovery |
| explain | explanation |
| important | importance |
| possible | possibility |
| successful | success |
| efficient | efficiency |

### Benefits of Nominalization

1. **More formal tone:**
   - Informal: We need to improve how we communicate.
   - Formal: There is a need for improved communication.

2. **Increased information density:**
   - Simple: The team performed well, which surprised everyone.
   - Nominalized: The team's excellent performance surprised everyone.

3. **Objective tone:**
   - Personal: I discovered that...
   - Impersonal: The discovery that...

### Examples

| Verbal Style | Nominalized Style |
|--------------|-------------------|
| We failed to complete the project. | The failure to complete the project... |
| It is important to conserve energy. | The importance of energy conservation... |
| Scientists have recently discovered... | Recent scientific discoveries... |
| The economy has grown rapidly. | Rapid economic growth... |

## Common Errors

### Confusing Word Pairs

| Incorrect | Correct | Explanation |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| affect / effect | The noise affects me. / The effect was immediate. | affect = verb, effect = noun (usually) |
| advice / advise | Give me advice. / I advise you to wait. | advice = noun, advise = verb |
| its / it's | The dog wagged its tail. / It's raining. | its = possessive, it's = it is |
| their / there / they're | Their house is there. They're happy. | their = possessive, there = place, they're = they are |
| your / you're | Your book. You're welcome. | your = possessive, you're = you are |
| who's / whose | Who's calling? Whose book is this? | who's = who is, whose = possessive |
| lose / loose | Don't lose it. The knot is loose. | lose = verb, loose = adjective |
| then / than | Better than before. Then we left. | than = comparison, then = time |
| lie / lay | Lie down. Lay the book here. | lie = no object, lay = needs object |
| rise / raise | Prices rise. Raise your hand. | rise = no object, raise = needs object |
| borrow / lend | Can I borrow your pen? Can you lend me a pen? | borrow = receive, lend = give |
| beside / besides | Sit beside me. Besides, I'm tired. | beside = next to, besides = in addition |
| accept / except | I accept your offer. Everyone except John. | accept = receive, except = exclude |
| principal / principle | The school principal. Basic principles. | principal = main/head, principle = rule |
| stationary / stationery | The car was stationary. Buy stationery. | stationary = not moving, stationery = paper/pens |

### Grammar Pitfalls

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|-------|------------|------|
| He suggested me to go. | He suggested (that) I go. | suggest + (that) + subject + base verb |
| I am agree. | I agree. | agree is not used with "be" |
| I am interesting in... | I am interested in... | -ed for feelings, -ing for causing |
| Despite of the rain... | Despite the rain... | despite + noun (no "of") |
| According to me... | In my opinion... | according to + other sources |
| I went to there. | I went there. | no "to" before "there/here" |
| Discuss about the issue. | Discuss the issue. | discuss + direct object (no "about") |
| Enter into the room. | Enter the room. | enter + direct object (no "into") |
| Explain me this. | Explain this to me. | explain something to someone |
| Comprise of... | Comprise... / Consist of... | comprise = include, consist of = be made of |
| The reason is because... | The reason is that... | reason + is + that (not because) |

### C1 Level Checklist

Use this to track your progress toward C1:

**Grammar:**
- [ ] All 12 tenses + perfect continuous forms
- [ ] Inversion after negative adverbials
- [ ] Cleft sentences (it-cleft, what-cleft)
- [ ] Participle clauses (present, past, perfect)
- [ ] Advanced relative clauses
- [ ] Subjunctive mood
- [ ] Causative structures
- [ ] Wish/If only constructions
- [ ] Mixed conditionals
- [ ] Modal perfects
- [ ] Ellipsis and substitution
- [ ] Discourse markers

**Vocabulary:**
- [ ] Academic Word List (570 word families)
- [ ] Advanced collocations
- [ ] Formal/informal register
- [ ] Word formation (prefixes, suffixes)
- [ ] Idiomatic expressions
- [ ] Phrasal verbs (100+)

**Skills:**
- [ ] Read complex texts on abstract topics
- [ ] Write clear, well-structured texts
- [ ] Express ideas fluently and spontaneously
- [ ] Use language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes
- [ ] Understand extended speech even when not clearly structured
- [ ] Understand most TV and films in standard dialect
